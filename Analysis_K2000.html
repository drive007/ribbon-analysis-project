Key Benefits

  • Significant Cost Reduction: Saves approximately 446,300 THB annually (~53.9%).
  • Streamlined Procurement: Simplifies ordering processes.
  • Technical Suitability (Type): As a Wax/Resin ribbon, K2000 is a better technical type match for the current Wax/Resin applications (8-394, 8-392) compared to Wax alternatives, reducing inherent durability risk.

Risks & Validation Needed

  • Performance Validation:
    • Must confirm K2000's print quality and durability are equivalent or superior to the current SATO T102E (Wax/Resin) on relevant materials (JP PACK, Gowns, SPT, OEM). Risk is considered Low due to matching type, but testing is crucial.
    • Must confirm K2000's performance is acceptable (potentially better) than the current BEA YI B220N (Wax) on its materials (Wraps, Masks).
  • Dimension Mismatch: (Medium Risk)
    • Consider optimizing size for suitability and potentially reducing price for 110mm applications (8-393). Requires validation.
    • Consider optimizing size for suitability and potentially reducing price for 85mm applications (8-392). Requires validation.
  • Settings Adjustment: K2000 may have different optimal heat/speed settings compared to current ribbons. Printers will need recalibration and testing.
  • Supplier Reliability: Ensure consistent supply and quality control from KURZ.

Conclusion & Recommendation

Consolidating to KURZ K2000 (117mm Wax/Resin) presents a compelling opportunity, offering substantial cost savings (~54%) while mitigating the critical durability risk associated with using Wax ribbons for Wax/Resin applications. The primary challenge is validating the performance across all materials and optimizing size/managing waste for narrower applications.

Recommendation: Prioritize rigorous testing of K2000 (117mm) across all current applications. Focus on durability tests for labels currently using SATO T102E and print quality tests for all. Investigate optimized sizing options with KURZ for 110mm and 85mm needs. If testing is successful, K2000 offers the best balance of cost savings and technical suitability.
