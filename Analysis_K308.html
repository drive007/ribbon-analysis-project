Key Benefits

  • Good Cost Reduction: Saves approximately 337,100 THB annually (~40.7%).
  • Streamlined Procurement: Simplifies ordering processes.
  • Potential Performance Upgrade: As a Premium Wax/Resin, K308 likely offers durability equal to or exceeding the current SATO T102E, potentially improving quality for demanding applications.
  • Low Durability Risk: Matching Wax/Resin type significantly reduces risk when replacing current Wax/Resin ribbons (8-394, 8-392).

Risks & Validation Needed

Risk is considered Low for Wax/Resin replacement, but testing against both current types is essential.

  • Cost vs. Benefit: While offering savings, K308 is more expensive than K2000. Need to determine if the potential premium performance justifies the lower savings compared to K2000 (~337k vs ~446k).
  • Settings Adjustment: K308 may require different optimal heat/speed settings compared to current ribbons. Printers will need recalibration and testing.
  • Supplier Reliability: Ensure consistent supply and quality control from KURZ.

Conclusion & Recommendation

Consolidating to KURZ K308 (117mm Wax/Resin Premium) offers a good balance of cost savings (~40.7%) and potentially enhanced performance with low durability risk for critical applications. Its main drawback compared to K2000 is the lower overall savings due to its higher price point.

Recommendation: K308 is a viable consolidation candidate, particularly if initial testing of K2000 reveals minor shortcomings in durability for the most demanding applications OR if a premium performance level is desired across the board.

  • Primary Testing (If K2000 shows issues): Test K308 (117mm) across all applications, focusing on comparing its durability against SATO T102E and its print quality against all current ribbons.
  • Secondary Testing (If K2000 is sufficient): K308 testing might be deprioritized if K2000 meets all requirements and offers better savings, unless specific applications warrant the premium quality.
  • Dimension Optimization: Investigate optimized sizing options with KURZ for 110mm and 85mm needs if proceeding with K308.
K308 represents a safe but less financially impactful option compared to K2000, best considered if K2000 doesn't fully meet performance needs during validation.
