Candidate Ribbon: SATO T102F (Wax)

This analysis evaluates replacing all current ribbon SKUs (8-394, 8-393, 8-392) with the SATO T102F ribbon, specifically using the 110mm x 300m variant (Test Product) for calculation.

Key Benefits

  • Significant Cost Reduction: Saves approximately 368,300 THB annually (~44.5%).
  • Streamlined Procurement: Potentially simplified ordering if SATO remains the supplier for this single SKU.
  • Reduced Storage Space: Requires less warehouse space.
  • Potential Future Bulk Discounts: Consolidating volume might enable better pricing later.

Risks & Validation Needed

  • Ink Type Mismatch (Durability): (Critical Risk)
    • T102F is a Wax ribbon. It fundamentally lacks the smudge, scratch, and chemical resistance of the Wax/Resin (SATO T102E) currently used for critical applications (8-394: JP PACK, Gowns; 8-392: SPT, OEM labels).
    • It is highly unlikely to be suitable for these applications without significant compromise on label durability. Rigorous testing is required, and acceptance of lower performance standards would be necessary.
  • Performance vs. Existing Wax:
    • Must confirm T102F's print quality and durability are equivalent or superior to the current BEA YI B220N (Wax) on its specific materials (Wraps, Masks - 8-393).
  • Dimension Mismatch: (Critical Risk - for 117mm / Medium Risk - for 85mm)
    • Consider optimizing size for suitability and potentially reducing price for 117mm applications (JP PACK, Gowns). Requires validation.
    • Consider optimizing size for suitability and potentially reducing price for 85mm applications (SPT, OEM). Requires validation.
  • Settings Adjustment: Melting point (70°C ± 5°C) may require different printer heat/speed settings compared to both current Wax/Resin and Wax ribbons. Needs testing and potential adjustments across all printers.
  • Supplier Reliability: Ensure SATO can consistently supply T102F in required volumes and maintain quality.

Conclusion & Recommendation

Consolidating to SATO T102F (110mm Wax) offers substantial potential cost savings (~44.5%). However, it presents critical risks related to durability (Wax replacing Wax/Resin) and significant dimension mismatches requiring size optimization and validation.

Recommendation: Due to the critical risks, full consolidation with T102F is highly problematic.

  • Primary Focus: Test T102F primarily as a potential replacement for the current Wax ribbon (8-393 BEA YI B220N) only. Confirm acceptable print quality and durability on Wraps/Masks.
  • Secondary Focus (High Risk): Only test T102F on current Wax/Resin applications (JP PACK, Gowns, SPT, OEM) if a significant reduction in durability is explicitly acceptable.
This approach focuses testing efforts where success is more likely (Wax for Wax replacement) while acknowledging the significant hurdles for replacing Wax/Resin applications.
